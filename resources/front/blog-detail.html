In the 21st century, hair loss problems are seen in women and men due to changes in weather conditions, differences in nutritional values, changes in living conditions and hereditary reasons. If hair loss, which is a common problem for many people in the world, is not treated, the hair roots are lost and cause baldness in the person. This problem is permanent; hair transplant solutions that make you smile, are practical, make your time valuable and do not cause pain are offered. The purpose of the hair transplant procedure is to help the person regain their healthy hair and increase their quality of life.

Not everyone is a suitable candidate for a hair transplant, as certain factors need to be considered to ensure the best possible outcome. Here's a guide to who is typically considered suitable for this procedure:

1. Degree of Hair Loss

Candidates for hair transplants often have significant, permanent hair loss. Male pattern baldness, female pattern baldness, and other types of genetic hair loss are common reasons people seek transplants. However, those experiencing temporary hair loss due to stress, medication, or certain medical conditions might not be ideal candidates until the underlying issue is resolved.

2. Age

Age can play a role in determining suitability. Hair loss patterns may not be fully developed in younger individuals, making it difficult to predict the future extent of hair loss. Therefore, hair transplants are typically recommended for adults who have stabilized hair loss, usually over the age of 25.

3. Donor Hair Availability

The success of a hair transplant depends on the availability of healthy donor hair. Individuals with sufficient hair on the back or sides of their scalp, which can be transplanted to balding areas, are considered good candidates. Those with extensive baldness and insufficient donor hair may not achieve the desired results.
